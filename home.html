About Us

Fighting Cybercrime Together

Our mission is to build a safer digital India

CyberShield is a Government of India initiative under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), designed to empower every citizen with the tools to identify and avoid cybercrime threats.

Every day, millions of Indians receive phishing links via SMS, email, and messaging apps. These malicious URLs mimic government portals, banks, and trusted services to steal personal data and money. CyberShield bridges the awareness gap by providing instant, reliable link verification.

Our verification engine cross-references submitted URLs against a live database of government-approved domains, known phishing patterns, suspicious TLDs, and malicious IP ranges — giving citizens a clear SAFE or UNSAFE verdict in seconds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about CyberShield

CyberShield is a government-backed web portal developed under the National Cyber Security Policy by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY). It is designed to help citizens verify the authenticity of links before clicking them, thus reducing phishing attacks, identity theft, and financial fraud.

When you paste a URL and click "Verify Link", our engine performs multiple checks:

  • Domain whitelist check — We compare the domain against our database of government-approved domains (e.g., .gov.in, .nic.in, mygov.in).
  • Phishing pattern analysis — We scan for common phishing tricks: IP-based URLs, suspicious TLDs (.xyz, .tk), lookalike domain names, and excessive subdomains.
  • Structural integrity check — We verify the URL structure for anomalies like homoglyph attacks or misleading paths.

Based on these checks, the URL is marked as Safe, Warning, or Unsafe.

Absolutely. CyberShield does not store, sell, or share the URLs you submit. All verifications are processed with end-to-end encryption (TLS 1.3). We do not collect personally identifiable information beyond what is necessary for account functionality. The platform complies with the IT Act 2000 and India's PDPB (Personal Data Protection Bill) guidelines.

A "Government Approved" link belongs to an official domain registered and verified by the Government of India or a recognized international government body. These include domains ending with:

  • .gov.in — Central/state government portals
  • .nic.in — National Informatics Centre
  • .gov — US and other international government sites
  • .gov.uk, .gov.au, etc. — Foreign government portals
  • Whitelisted portals like mygov.in, digilocker.gov.in, irctc.co.in

If you suspect you've clicked a phishing link, take these steps immediately:

  1. Do NOT enter any personal information on the page.
  2. Disconnect your device from the internet if possible.
  3. Change passwords for any accounts that may be compromised.
  4. Report the incident to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in.
  5. Contact your bank if financial information was involved.
  6. Run a full antivirus scan on your device.
